# Frameworks

## Democratization With Guardrails

Distribute standard experiment execution and decisions to product teams. Centralize assignment reliability, metric definitions, diagnostics, documentation standards, and support for difficult designs.

## Constraint-Led Infrastructure

Identify the current bottleneck before adding platform capability. Automate recurring work and known failure modes; avoid designing for speculative volume or organization shapes.

## Concurrent-Test Design

Permit ordinary overlap when mechanisms are independent and exposure is observable. Evaluate concurrency through plausible interaction, shared surfaces, scarce resources, safety, and decision consequences.

## Interaction Avoidance and Detection

Use mutual exclusion for direct conflicts or unacceptable risk. Otherwise log concurrent exposure, predefine important interaction checks, and investigate meaningful heterogeneity without searching every possible combination.

## Scientist Versus Bandit

Use controlled experiments when causal inference and reusable learning dominate. Consider adaptive allocation when cumulative reward matters and its assumptions, measurement, and interpretability costs are acceptable.

## Smaller-Question Evidence Design

For limited traffic, test larger changes, narrow the population and decision, improve measurement, reduce variance, lengthen the window where appropriate, and combine experiments with qualitative or operational evidence.

## Empowered-Team Ownership

Product teams own customer problems, hypotheses, outcomes, and actions. The central experimentation function owns shared capabilities, coaching, quality, and learning synthesis rather than becoming a request queue.

## Leadership and Evidence Culture

Audit whether leaders act on negative evidence, tolerate challenges to intuition, reward learning, and distribute capability beyond individual sponsors.
