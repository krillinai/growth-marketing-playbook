# Activation

> Activation is the first evidence that a new user has reached meaningful product value. It is a behavioral hypothesis linked to later retention, not completion of an onboarding interface.

**[English](README.md)｜[简体中文](README.zh.md)**

## Core Questions

- What outcome proves that the user received value?
- Which early behavior predicts retained use?
- How should different intents receive different paths?

## Cross-Company Patterns

Effective systems connect product value, target behavior, measurement horizon, and economic or quality guardrails. Surface tactics differ, but early signals still need validation against durable value.

## Company Evidence

| Company or person | Evidence and approach |
| --- | --- |
| Airtable | Segmented onboarding, templates, useful first workflows, and week-four collaboration. |
| Facebook / Meta | Friend connections and the Magic Moment as early value proxies. |
| Sean Ellis | Must-have value, Aha Moments, and activation as the bridge to retention. |

## Key Metrics

- Time to first value
- Activation rate by segment and source
- Week-one and week-four retained activation
- Depth, breadth, or collaboration after activation
- Errors, abandonment, and support guardrails

## Transferable Method

1. Interview retained users and inspect their first successful path.
2. Define one observable behavior hypothesized to predict retained value.
3. Segment users only when the product path changes.
4. Validate the relationship through cohorts and guardrails.

## What Not to Copy Directly

- Do not copy a universal Aha Moment or activation threshold.
- Do not treat wizard completion, tooltip dismissal, or an invitation sent as delivered value.

- Company evidence relies mainly on participant interviews and public material, not complete internal causal evaluation.

- Publisher growth and revenue claims are not used to prove individual methods.
