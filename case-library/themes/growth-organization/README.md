# Growth Organization

> Growth organization defines ownership, decision rights, cross-functional access, and learning systems around growth constraints. The goal is not one universal org chart but coordinated accountability for customer value.

**[English](README.md)｜[简体中文](README.zh.md)**

## Core Questions

- Which constraint needs a dedicated owner?
- Which functions must change product, distribution, data, or economics together?
- What context and authority let teams decide quickly without losing control?

## Cross-Company Patterns

A horizontal capability matters only when it connects to a specific growth stage and customer value. Mature systems design process, ownership, data, feedback, and risk boundaries together.

## Company Evidence

| Company | Evidence and approach |
| --- | --- |
| Ramp | Focused autonomy, growth engineering, experiment portfolios, writing, and complementary talent. |
| ByteDance | UG as a shared platform with local execution, automation, and risk separation. |
| Facebook / Meta | Cross-functional growth teams, instrumentation, product access, and shared retention goals. |
| Lovable | Employee participation in distribution and close connection between building and communication. |

## Key Metrics

- Decision and learning cycle time
- Cross-functional dependency wait
- Experiment and launch follow-through
- Customer-value outcome by team
- Quality, incident, and sustainability guardrails

## Transferable Method

1. Name the current constraint before choosing an org shape.
2. Give teams context, product access, clear outcomes, and explicit decision rights.
3. Centralize shared capabilities while preserving market and product judgment.
4. Use reviews and postmortems to improve the operating system, not assign blame.

## What Not to Copy Directly

- Do not hire growth to manufacture product-market fit.
- Do not copy a large-company platform team or require every employee to become a marketer.
